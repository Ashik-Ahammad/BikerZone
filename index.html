Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Always prioritize safety by wearing a properly fitted helmet and obeying traffic laws. Regularly inspect your bike for wear and tear, particularly brakes, tires, and chains. Maintain proper tire inflation and lubrication. Practice basic riding skills and be aware of your surroundings.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To determine if your helmet needs replacement, check the manufacturing date (most have a 5-10 year lifespan) and inspect for cracks, dents, or other damage. If involved in a crash, replace it regardless of visible damage. Remember that the foam inside can degrade over time, affecting its ability to absorb impact.

My bike has been in store is it safe to ride?

After storing your bike, check tire pressure, inspect and test brakes, lubricate the chain if necessary, and tighten any loose parts. Take a short test ride to ensure everything is functioning correctly.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Follow the same traffic laws as cars, including stopping at signals and using hand signals for turns and lane changes. Ride in the direction of traffic and utilize bike lanes when available. Maintain a straight line and avoid weaving between vehicles.
